Protect yourself when medical savings run out

The MSA is a percentage of the contribution that is set aside for day-to-day medical expenses such as doctors' consultations and prescriptions. While the initial amount in the MSA might sound like a lot of money, it's inclined to disappear very quickly. Medical care is expensive, and savings are depleted quickly.

Unfortunately, medical needs don't stop at the same time that savings run dry. Medical aid members now face the challenge of continuing to pay their contributions while funding medical expenses as well.

What are your options?

Explore your benefits

In addition to medical treatments and savings accounts, many medical aids offer preventive care that the scheme funds. For example, you might be entitled to a Pap smear, PSA test and flu vaccine dose, among others. Making use of these benefits makes the early diagnosis of medical conditions possible. Early intervention will cost less than the price of advanced stage.

Know what you're entitled to

While you still have savings, be clear on what is going to be deducted from them. For example, some medical aids deduct the cost of radiology and pathology from the savings account, while others do not. When you receive the benefit guidelines for the year, read through it carefully. This strategy allows you to budget to make sure you aren't going to deplete your savings too soon.

Treatment of PMB conditions

Prescribed minimum benefits (PMBs) dictate that certain conditions must be treated whether you have savings or not. It includes several common medical conditions such as diabetes and hypertension. Consultations for these conditions will be paid for despite a lack of savings provided they are billed correctly by the consulting doctor.

Child benefits

Your medical aid may offer payment for consultations for children even if you've depleted your savings. The coverage varies from one medical aid to another. Refer to the benefits guide or contact your medical aid directly.

Chronic medication

Depending on your plan, your approved chronic medication should be deducted from a separate fund. Make sure you and your doctor have filled in the necessary paperwork. If you don't, chronic medication costs are deducted from your savings.

Upgrading your plan

You might want to investigate upgrading your medical aid plan to get access to more savings. However, the cost of your contribution will increase. Weigh up the extra money you'll pay against the added savings. It might be worth your while to invest the difference in a savings account of your own.

Generic medication

Always check whether your prescribed medication has a cheaper generic version. Use generics whenever possible, while you have savings and after you don't. It saves you money either way.

The Pharmacy Price War Of 2019

ðŸ’Š

The rising cost of private healthcare and crippled public health service makes it necessary for South Africans to take care of their health. It's too expensive to get sick in South Africa unless you can brave the long queues and non-availability of staff and medicines at clinics and hospitals.

The face of the South African pharmacy has changed drastically in the last 20 years. The advent of the chain store pharmacy has made it difficult for privately-owned pharmacy owners to keep their businesses afloat. This has led to what many are calling the "Pharmacy Price War of 2019."

How the Price War Affects Consumers

Increased Competition

With major retail chains entering the pharmaceutical market, consumers now have more options than ever before. This competition has driven prices down on many common medications, making healthcare more accessible to the average South African.

Convenience vs. Personalized Service

While chain pharmacies offer convenience and lower prices, many independent pharmacies provide more personalized service. They often know their customers by name and can offer tailored advice about medications and health conditions.

Impact on Quality

Some experts worry that the focus on price competition might compromise the quality of service. However, all pharmacies in South Africa must adhere to strict regulatory standards, ensuring that medications are dispensed safely regardless of where you purchase them.

Tips for Smart Pharmacy Shopping

Compare Prices

Don't assume all pharmacies charge the same prices. Take the time to compare prices for your regular medications at different pharmacies in your area. You might be surprised at the differences.

Ask About Generic Alternatives

Generic medications contain the same active ingredients as brand-name drugs but are typically much cheaper. Always ask your doctor or pharmacist if a generic alternative is available for your prescription.

Check Your Medical Aid Benefits

Some medical aid plans have preferred pharmacy networks that offer better pricing. Check with your medical aid to see if you can save money by using specific pharmacies.

Consider Mail Order Pharmacies

For chronic medications, mail order pharmacies can often offer significant savings. These services deliver your medications directly to your door, saving you time and potentially money.

The pharmacy price war of 2019 has ultimately benefited South African consumers by making medications more affordable. However, it's important to balance price considerations with the quality of service and advice you receive from your pharmacist.
